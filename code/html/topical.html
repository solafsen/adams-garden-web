Universal Design

Process decription

In the process of working with this third assignment, we first created one fictional character each with the characteristics listed in Step 1. After presenting our personas to each other with theatrical engagement, we made a hybrid person with characteristics from the original three characters, as distinctive from ourselves as possible.

Carlo's life and relevant background information

The person we ended up describing is Carlos. Carlos is a 59-year-old male from Havana, Cuba. He works as a taxi driver in the capital city and works from sunrise to sunset. He lives a happy life with his wife (Yolanda, 52 years) and three children (Raul 30, Klara 27, and Manuel 25 years) in a small apartment in one of the suburbs of Havana. Carlos loves to listen to RnB music, dance with his wife Yolanda and spend time cooking with his family. He dislikes

Ten barriers to the mobile phone and how they may affect Carlos

We chose the mobile smartphone as our piece of modern technology. Some barriers are related to the design of the mobile phone. These include battery life, screen and text size and the complexity and cost of the device. Some barriers are related to functionality. These could be the cost and accessibility of data, the time consumption and stress related to this and fear of theft or damage to the phone or loss of personal information (Silver et al., 2019).

Solutions to the barriers and how they can benefit Carlos

As a taxi driver, Carlos is constantly on the go and does not have regular access to electricity for charging his phone. He has difficulties seeing the screen with normal text size and finds it hard to navigate on the smartphone without traditional buttons. Even though Carlos is hard working, he does not have a high income compared to western standards. The cost of buying a mobile phone device is significant, and the data cost comes in addition. The internet access in Cuba is limited, thus the functionality and usage of the mobile phone is reduced in this area. Carlos is stressed about using too much data, as the cost could be too much for him to handle. He is also scared of being unreachable if something happens with his family or friends. Since the mobile phone is such an expensive device, Carlos is worried he might damage or lose his phone (due to theft or misplacement) and what might happen to his personal data whenever this occurs.

Carlos has difficulties maneuvering his phone, but he also has the option to increase text size to see the text more clearly and he could also utilize the screen reader and voice recognition features to reduce touch screen activity. If Nikolao Tesla vision (Vujic et al., 2001) of free, unlimited access to wireless electricity all over the world was implemented, several of Carlos`s problems regarding his mobile phone would be solved, especially stress and cost related to phone charging and internet accessibility. To combat the problem of expensive phones, we propose a recycling solution where “old” discarded phones from developed countries are fixed and utilized in poorer populations like in Cuba, so the expenses of buying a mobile phone will be reduced significantly. With a cheaper phone, Carlos is not as stressed with losing or damaging it, so he can focus more on keeping his wife happy. To reduce his fear of losing personal data, Carlos could for instance take a course in data security and change his password from “Yolanda” to “y0L@nDa1970T3@Mo” and make sure to change it quite frequently
